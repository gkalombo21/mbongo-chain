# 🌍 Mbongo-Chain Code of Conduct

## 1. Our Pledge

We, as members, contributors, and leaders of the **Mbongo-Chain community**, pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- Age  
- Body size  
- Visible or invisible disability  
- Ethnicity  
- Gender identity and expression  
- Level of experience  
- Nationality  
- Personal appearance  
- Race  
- Religion or belief  
- Sexual identity and orientation  

We are committed to acting and interacting in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## 2. Our Standards

**Examples of positive behavior include:**

- Showing empathy and kindness toward other community members.  
- Respecting different opinions and constructive feedback.  
- Gracefully accepting responsibility for mistakes and learning from them.  
- Focusing on what is best for the community.  
- Supporting new contributors and helping them grow.  

**Examples of unacceptable behavior include:**

- Use of sexualized language or imagery, or sexual attention or advances.  
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.  
- Public or private harassment.  
- Publishing others’ private information (e.g., physical or email address) without permission.  
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

---

## 3. Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards.  
They may take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.  

Leaders have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

---

## 4. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within:

- All project spaces (repositories, issues, discussions, pull requests).  
- Public channels where the project or its community is represented.  
- Any interactions representing the project officially (online or offline).

---

## 5. Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the Mbongo-Chain Community Team:

- **Email:** `conduct@mbongo-chain.org`  
- **Subject line:** `Code of Conduct Violation Report`

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.  
The team is obligated to maintain confidentiality regarding the reporter of an incident.

---

## 6. Enforcement Guidelines

The community leaders will follow these guidelines when determining consequences for violations:

| Level | Impact | Consequence |
|:--|:--|:--|
| 1 | Minor | Warning and education |
| 2 | Moderate | Temporary restriction of participation |
| 3 | Severe | Permanent removal from community spaces |
| 4 | Critical | Legal action or ban from all project activities |

## 8. Commitment

By participating in the Mbongo-Chain project, you agree to:

- Uphold these principles.  
- Treat others with dignity and respect.  
- Help create a positive, collaborative, and global open-source ecosystem.
